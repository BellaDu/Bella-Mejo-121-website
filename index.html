Family Story


Since I remember, our house seemed to have no roof. If you open our door on weekend evenings, the deafening noise of chatting, cheering, laughing, and singing will blow you away. That's my aunt pinching my cousin's ears, urging him to fix her Alipay, my uncle assuring his wife with his charming bass that he would be forever faithful, and my grandpa acting Guan Gong's saga in The Romance of The Three Kingdoms, surrounded by his old buddies. It was in such a rowdy place that I learned how to tell a story, to put up a show, and to ask for red envelopes in a hundred ways. The desire to communicate is in our blood; it renders us with endless courage and enthusiasm for life.
